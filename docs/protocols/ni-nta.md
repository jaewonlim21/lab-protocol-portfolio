# Ni-NTA Purification

## Overview
Batch affinity purification workflow for His-tagged protein samples.

## Purpose
To bind, wash, and elute target protein using Ni-NTA resin.

## Procedure
### A. Resin preparation
1. Gently resuspend Ni-NTA resin.
2. Aliquot resin into labeled tubes.
3. Spin at low speed and discard storage supernatant.
4. Equilibrate resin with equilibration buffer.

### B. Binding
1. Adjust lysate with equilibration buffer.
2. Add equilibrated resin.
3. Rotate for at least 30 minutes or overnight at 4°C.

### C. Post-binding
1. Spin down resin.
2. Collect unbound fraction.

### D. Wash
1. Add wash buffer.
2. Spin and collect wash fractions.
3. Repeat based on sample type.

### E. Elution
1. Add elution buffer.
2. Spin and collect E1.
3. Repeat and collect E2.

## Critical parameters
- fully equilibrate resin before binding
- keep fraction labeling consistent
- distinguish pellet-derived and supernatant-derived workflows
- compare unbound, wash, and elution fractions by gel

## Troubleshooting
### Protein remains in unbound fraction
Binding conditions may be suboptimal or the sample may require more compatible buffer conditions.

### Elution is weak
Review imidazole concentration, buffer composition, and resin condition.

### Wash fractions contain too much protein
Binding may be incomplete or wash conditions too stringent.

## Notes from experience
This page is a good place to add real purification judgment: whether E1 is stronger than E2, whether re-purification may be needed, and whether concentration should happen after fraction review.
